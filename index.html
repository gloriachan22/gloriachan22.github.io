Geotechnical-Led Masterplanning

Shell Centre Redevelopment

RIBA Stage C redevelopment proposal for the Shell Centre, London, adopting a geotechnical-led masterplanning approach. The project focused on construction feasibility above sensitive underground infrastructure and on maximising reuse of existing foundations within a constrained urban site.

  • Site constrained by Bakerloo and Northern Line tunnels and existing piled foundations
  • Ground model developed using published Waterloo–Thames stratigraphy and groundwater data
  • 100% pile reuse strategy developed to minimise ground disturbance and embodied carbon
  • Staged construction modelled in PLAXIS to predict settlement and tunnel response
  • Protective measures designed to limit displacement of underground assets

ABAQUS Finite Element Analysis

Nonlinear Buckling Behaviour of Thin Cylindrical Shells

Investigation of elastic, geometric, and material nonlinear effects on shell buckling and imperfection sensitivity under axial compression.

  • Compared GNA, GNIA, and fully coupled GMNIA workflows
  • Showed that idealised linear and bifurcation analyses overestimate shell capacity
  • Studied the influence of geometric imperfections on early buckling and stiffness loss
  • Captured collapse behaviour governed by both instability and material yielding
